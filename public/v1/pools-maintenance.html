Pool Care Guide

Swimming Pool Maintenance in Dubai

Everything you need to know about keeping your pool clean, safe, and sparkling in the UAE climate.

Owning a pool in Dubai is a luxury, but it comes with responsibility. The UAE's extreme heat, sand, and humidity create unique challenges that require consistent, knowledgeable maintenance. Neglect your pool for even a week in summer and you could be looking at green water, clogged filters, and costly repairs.

This guide covers everything from weekly checklists to seasonal strategies, so whether you maintain your pool yourself or hire a professional, you will know exactly what needs to happen and when.

Weekly Checklist

  • Test water pH (ideal: 7.2 to 7.6)
  • Test chlorine levels (ideal: 1 to 3 ppm)
  • Skim surface debris and leaves
  • Empty skimmer and pump baskets
  • Brush walls and tile line
  • Vacuum pool floor
  • Check water level (mid skimmer)
  • Inspect pump and filter pressure

Monthly Tasks

  • Backwash or clean filter media
  • Test alkalinity (80 to 120 ppm)
  • Test calcium hardness (200 to 400 ppm)
  • Inspect pool equipment for leaks
  • Clean salt cell (if applicable)
  • Check and lubricate O-rings
  • Test TDS levels
  • Inspect pool lights and fittings
Seasonal Care

Dubai Seasonal Pool Tips

Summer (Jun to Sep)

Dubai summers push water temps above 35C. Run your chiller, increase chlorine dosage by 20%, and clean filters more frequently. Algae grows faster in heat.

Winter (Nov to Feb)

Pool usage drops but maintenance should not. Reduce pump run time to 6 hours, lower chemical dosage slightly, and keep the pool covered when not in use to prevent debris.

Sand Storm Season

After a sandstorm, run the pump for an extra cycle, vacuum the floor, backwash the filter, and shock treat the water. Sand carries contaminants that spike turbidity.

When to Call a Professional

Some issues need expert hands. Call us if you notice any of these:

Water stays cloudy despite chemical treatment
Visible cracks or tile damage in the pool shell
Pump making unusual noises or losing prime
Unexplained water level drop (possible leak)
Green water that does not respond to shock treatment
Equipment error codes or automation failures
Filter pressure consistently high after backwash
Staining on pool walls or floor
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I test my pool water in Dubai?

At minimum, test pH and chlorine twice a week during summer and once a week in winter. A full chemical panel (alkalinity, calcium, TDS) should be done monthly.

Why does my pool turn green so fast in summer?

Dubai's extreme heat accelerates algae growth. Ensure your chlorine stays at 2 to 3 ppm, run the pump for 8 to 10 hours daily, and brush walls weekly to prevent algae from taking hold.

Can I maintain my pool myself or should I hire a professional?

Basic weekly skimming and chemical testing can be done yourself. However, for equipment servicing, filter maintenance, and leak detection, a professional service ensures nothing is missed.

How much does professional pool maintenance cost in Dubai?

Monthly maintenance plans in Dubai typically range from AED 500 to AED 1,500 depending on pool size and frequency of visits. Contact us for a custom quote.

What is the ideal pool temperature in Dubai?

Most residents prefer 26C to 28C. In summer, a pool chiller is essential. In winter, a heat pump can extend your swimming season comfortably.
